About Me

Hello! Wow, somehow, you've landed here to learn more about me. I'm thrilled! For starters, I'm gearing up to make a career change. I'm currently in a successful sales career, but looking for something more hands on. What I enjoy most about my current career is the challange, working with a team and solving problems. I'm hoping to continue this in a career with software development.

Naturally, I've always been the kind of person who likes to work on a project (or skill) and see the outcome. My most recent project is converting a 1992 Ford Mustang with a 4cyl motor into a 5.0 Mustang. I could have bought one, but I wouldn't have learned how to:

  • Remove and replace a motor, transmission and exhaust.
  • Replace clutch and brake cable.
  • Upgrade the front and rear brakes (calipers, pads, rotors, spindles and all).
  • Replace front and rear suspension parts with:
    • Lowering springs.
    • New struts and bushings.
    • Heavier duty rear control arms.
  • Replace the fuel lines and brake lines.
  • Replace the rear axel, suspension and struts.
  • Replace engine wiring harness, transmition harness and computer module.
  • Replace gauge cluster.

The subject here doesn't directly correlate with computer programming, but the idea of this does. I did this all by reading forum after forum after forum and teaching myself how to do everything. It's a great feeling of accomplishment and confidence to know I can learn and teach myself with hard work and dedication.

Aside from this, my wife and I are expecting a baby girl very soon and live with our 2 Boxers - all of which I couldn't be any happier with. I also enjoy cooking, craft beer, music and exercise.
